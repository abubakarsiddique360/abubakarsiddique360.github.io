Consciousness

Consciousness is Fundamental

Federico Faggin argues consciousness is woven into reality, not produced by the brain.

Federico Faggin, the designer of the Intel 4004 microprocessor, now explores the nature of consciousness. His theory of Quantum Information Panpsychism argues that consciousness is not a by-product of the brain but a foundational element of the universe.

In Faggin's view, our bodies are like sophisticated drones operated by a conscious quantum field.

The part-whole: cells vs microprocessors

Faggin draws a distinction between living cells and transistors. Cells are part-whole entities that contain potential knowledge of the organism and communicate through quantum fields.

Microprocessors are classical machines that process bits deterministically with no inner awareness. This leads him to challenge the purely materialist view of intelligence.

Free will and the collapse of the wave function

In quantum physics, the collapse of the wave function is when probabilities become a definite state. Faggin sees this as the instant of free will - the universe making a choice.

Meaning, not just information

Meaning resides in consciousness, not in raw information. A book has symbols, but meaning appears only when consciousness engages with it. This reframes the AI debate: data processing is not understanding.

The next frontier is understanding consciousness, not just building smarter machines.
